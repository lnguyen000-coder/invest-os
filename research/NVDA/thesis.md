# NVIDIA Corporation (NVDA) — Investment Thesis


## Thesis in one paragraph
NVIDIA has established itself as the leading provider of accelerated computing infrastructure for artificial intelligence, combining industry-leading GPUs, networking, software, and developer tools into a tightly integrated platform. I believe the market sometimes focuses too heavily on short-term AI spending cycles while underestimating the durability of NVIDIA's ecosystem, software lock-in, and expanding opportunities beyond training large language models into inference, enterprise AI, robotics, autonomous systems, and accelerated computing. My investment thesis depends on NVIDIA maintaining its technology leadership and continuing to convert AI adoption into long-term free cash flow growth.

## Moat
CUDA software ecosystem

CUDA is arguably NVIDIA's strongest competitive advantage. Years of developer adoption, optimized libraries, and enterprise workflows create meaningful switching costs beyond the hardware itself.

Hardware + software integration

NVIDIA designs chips, networking, system architecture, developer tools, and AI software as one integrated platform.

Developer ecosystem

Millions of developers, researchers, and enterprises have built AI applications around NVIDIA's software stack.

Scale

Its enormous cash generation allows NVIDIA to invest aggressively in next-generation architectures, networking, software, and manufacturing partnerships.

Customer relationships

Hyperscalers, enterprises, governments, and research organizations increasingly build their AI infrastructure around NVIDIA.

## What I'm paying for growth
I am willing to pay a premium valuation if:

AI infrastructure demand remains structurally strong.
CUDA continues increasing switching costs.
Gross margins remain exceptional.
Free cash flow compounds rapidly.
Enterprise AI adoption expands beyond hyperscalers.

I become cautious if:

AI infrastructure spending becomes cyclical rather than structural.
Customers meaningfully diversify away from NVIDIA.
Gross margins compress structurally.
Valuation assumes unrealistic long-term growth.

## Kill criteria (what would make me sell)
NVIDIA loses its technology leadership in AI acceleration.
CUDA becomes materially less important to developers.
Enterprise customers successfully migrate to competing ecosystems at scale.
Gross margins deteriorate because competitive pressures permanently increase.
AI infrastructure demand proves materially weaker than expected over several years.
My original investment thesis is no longer valid.

## Known risks
Customer concentration among large cloud providers.
Increasing competition from AMD, custom AI chips, and hyperscaler-designed silicon.
Export restrictions affecting international sales.
AI infrastructure spending slowing after an initial investment cycle.
Supply chain dependence.
High market expectations embedded in the valuation.
Regulatory and geopolitical risks.
