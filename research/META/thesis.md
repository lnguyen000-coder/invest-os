# Meta Platforms Inc. (META) — Investment Thesis



## Thesis in one paragraph
Meta owns some of the world's largest digital platforms—Facebook, Instagram, WhatsApp, Messenger, and Threads—and has built one of the most effective advertising businesses ever created. I believe the market sometimes focuses too heavily on short-term advertising cycles or Reality Labs spending while underestimating Meta's ability to improve monetization through AI, messaging, business tools, and creator ecosystems. With billions of daily users, exceptional cash generation, and a founder willing to invest for the long term, Meta has multiple paths to compound intrinsic value over the next decade.

## Moat
Unmatched user scale

Billions of people use at least one Meta platform regularly, making it difficult for advertisers and creators to ignore.

Network effects

The value of Facebook, Instagram, WhatsApp, and Threads increases as more users, businesses, and creators participate.

Advertising platform

Meta combines massive reach with sophisticated AI-driven targeting and measurement, creating strong returns for advertisers.

AI capabilities

Meta uses AI to improve recommendations, advertising efficiency, content ranking, and creator discovery while also developing open-source AI models that strengthen its ecosystem.

Financial strength

Meta generates enormous free cash flow, allowing it to invest aggressively in AI infrastructure while continuing share repurchases.

## What I'm paying for growth
I am comfortable paying a premium if:

Advertising continues growing.
AI improves advertiser return on investment.
User engagement remains strong.
Messaging becomes a larger revenue contributor.
Free cash flow continues compounding.

I become cautious if:

User engagement weakens materially.
Advertising pricing deteriorates structurally.
AI investments fail to generate attractive returns.
Reality Labs spending permanently destroys shareholder value without strategic benefits.

## Kill criteria (what would make me sell)
Meta's platforms experience sustained declines in user engagement.
Advertising effectiveness weakens significantly relative to competitors.
AI investments fail to strengthen the core business over time.
Management continues investing heavily in projects with persistently poor returns and no credible strategic rationale.
Regulatory changes materially impair Meta's ability to monetize its platforms.
My original investment thesis is no longer valid.

## Known risks
Antitrust and regulatory action.
Privacy regulation affecting advertising.
Competition for user attention from emerging platforms.
AI increasing infrastructure costs faster than revenue.
Reality Labs investments failing to generate acceptable returns.
Changes in consumer behavior.
Platform-specific reputational risks.
