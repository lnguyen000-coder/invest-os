## Thesis in one paragraph
Microsoft is one of the highest-quality businesses in the world with multiple durable competitive advantages across enterprise software, cloud infrastructure, AI, cybersecurity, and developer tools. I believe the market still underestimates Microsoft's ability to monetize AI because it already owns the distribution channels—Microsoft 365, Windows, GitHub, Azure, Dynamics, and LinkedIn—rather than needing to build new ones. Even if AI adoption takes longer than expected, the core business generates enormous recurring cash flow, allowing Microsoft to continue investing while returning capital to shareholders. This is a business I would be comfortable owning for the next decade.

## Moat
Switching costs

Enterprise customers rarely replace Microsoft 365, Active Directory, Azure, SQL Server, or Windows because of the cost, complexity, and operational risk.

Ecosystem

Customers increasingly buy multiple Microsoft products together, creating higher switching costs and cross-selling opportunities.

Scale

Massive global cloud infrastructure enables Microsoft to compete effectively with Amazon and Google while maintaining attractive margins.

Distribution

Microsoft already has hundreds of millions of business users, making AI features easier to monetize than competitors without an installed base.

Capital

Huge free cash flow allows Microsoft to invest aggressively in AI, data centers, acquisitions, and cybersecurity.

## Kill criteria (what would make me sell)
1. Azure loses meaningful cloud market share for multiple years
2. AI monetization consistently fails despite heavy investment
3. Enterprise customer retention weakens materially
4. Management begins making poor capital allocation decisions
5. Free cash flow growth stalls for several years without a compelling explanation
6. Competitive position weakens against AWS, Google, or emerging AI platforms
7. Investment thesis changes - not simply because the stock price declines
