# Palantir Tech Inc. (PLTR) — Investment Thesis



## Thesis in one paragraph
Palantir is building the operating system for enterprise and government decision-making by integrating fragmented data, AI models, and operational workflows into a single platform. I believe the market often focuses on quarterly valuation while underestimating the long-term value of becoming deeply embedded in mission-critical operations. As Artificial Intelligence Platform (AIP) adoption grows, Palantir has the potential to become core infrastructure for organizations rather than another software vendor. While the valuation may periodically become expensive, I believe the quality of the business and its long runway justify owning it over many years, provided execution continues.

## Moat
High switching costs

Once Palantir's software becomes integrated into an organization's operations, replacing it is difficult because it becomes intertwined with data pipelines, workflows, and decision-making processes.

Deep customer integration

Palantir works alongside customers to solve complex operational problems rather than simply selling software licenses, creating strong customer relationships and increasing the likelihood of expansion.

Government relationships

Years of trusted work with defense, intelligence, and government agencies create barriers to entry that are difficult for competitors to replicate.

AI operating layer

Rather than building foundation models, Palantir focuses on orchestrating models, enterprise data, permissions, and workflows. This model-agnostic approach allows customers to adopt new AI models without rebuilding their entire software stack.

Brand and credibility

Palantir has developed a reputation for solving highly complex problems where traditional enterprise software often falls short.

## What I'm paying for growth
Palantir almost always trades at a premium valuation because investors expect exceptional long-term growth.

I am comfortable paying a premium only if:

Commercial revenue continues to accelerate.
AIP adoption translates into durable revenue growth.
Free cash flow margins remain strong.
Existing customers continue expanding their usage.
Management demonstrates disciplined execution.

I become cautious if:

Revenue growth slows significantly while the valuation remains elevated.
AI enthusiasm drives the share price far ahead of business fundamentals.
Profitability deteriorates without a clear strategic reason.

## Kill criteria (what would make me sell)
Sell or materially reduce my position if:

Commercial growth stalls for several consecutive years.
Customer expansion weakens significantly.
AIP fails to generate meaningful long-term revenue.
Management repeatedly misses execution expectations.
Stock-based compensation becomes excessive again.
Free cash flow deteriorates structurally.
A superior platform meaningfully erodes Palantir's competitive position.
My original investment thesis is no longer valid.

Do not sell simply because of volatility or a sharp share price decline if the underlying business remains strong.

## Known risks
Extremely high valuation expectations.
Dependence on continued AI adoption.
Slower government spending.
Long enterprise sales cycles.
Customer concentration.
Increasing competition from hyperscalers and enterprise software providers.
Political or regulatory changes affecting government contracts.
Dilution from stock-based compensation.
