# Amazon Com Inc. (AMZN) — Investment Thesis


## Thesis in one paragraph
Amazon is one of the world's strongest long-term compounders, combining leadership in e-commerce, cloud computing, digital advertising, logistics, AI infrastructure, and subscription services. I believe the market often values Amazon based on near-term retail margins while underestimating the long-term earnings power created by AWS, advertising, Prime, and AI. As operating efficiency improves and higher-margin businesses become a larger share of revenue, Amazon has the potential to grow free cash flow substantially over the next decade. I am investing in Amazon because I believe it will continue finding new ways to deploy capital into large, attractive markets while strengthening its competitive advantages.

## Moat
Scale

Amazon's scale creates advantages in purchasing, fulfillment, logistics, cloud infrastructure, and technology investment that are difficult for competitors to replicate.

Logistics network

Its fulfillment and delivery network allows Amazon to provide fast, reliable shipping while lowering costs over time through higher utilization and automation.

Prime ecosystem

Prime increases customer loyalty by combining shipping, entertainment, exclusive offers, and other benefits into a subscription that encourages repeat purchases.

AWS

Amazon Web Services is deeply embedded within enterprise technology stacks. Customers face significant switching costs due to application architecture, data migration, and operational complexity.

Advertising platform

Amazon's retail marketplace gives it access to high-intent shopping data, making its advertising business particularly valuable for brands seeking measurable returns.

Capital allocation

Amazon has repeatedly demonstrated an ability to reinvest cash flow into new businesses that become meaningful profit drivers.

## What I'm paying for growth
I am willing to pay a premium because Amazon has multiple high-quality businesses with long growth runways.

The valuation is justified if:

AWS maintains strong growth and profitability.
Advertising continues growing faster than retail.
AI infrastructure investments generate attractive returns.
Retail margins continue improving through automation and scale.
Free cash flow grows faster than revenue.

I become cautious if:

AWS growth slows materially.
Capital expenditures fail to generate corresponding earnings growth.
Retail profitability deteriorates without a strategic reason.
Valuation assumes unrealistic growth expectations.

## Kill criteria (what would make me sell)
AWS loses meaningful market share over several years.
Advertising growth weakens structurally.
Free cash flow stagnates despite continued heavy investment.
Management begins allocating capital inefficiently.
Retail economics worsen permanently rather than temporarily.
Amazon loses its culture of long-term innovation.
My original investment thesis is no longer valid.
