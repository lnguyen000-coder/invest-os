# AST SpaceMobile Inc. (ASTS) — Investment Thesis



## Thesis in one paragraph
AST SpaceMobile aims to build the first global, space-based cellular broadband network that connects directly to ordinary smartphones without requiring specialized hardware. If successful, the company could create a new communications infrastructure serving underserved regions, emergency connectivity, and partner mobile operators worldwide. The investment case depends far more on execution than on demand: proving the technology works reliably, scaling satellite deployment, securing financing, and converting partnerships into meaningful recurring revenue. While the upside could be substantial, the company remains pre-mature and carries significant execution, financing, and competitive risks.

## Moat
Direct-to-device technology

If ASTS continues demonstrating reliable direct-to-smartphone connectivity at scale, it may establish a technological lead in a difficult engineering domain.

Spectrum and operator partnerships

Relationships with mobile network operators can create distribution advantages because customers can use their existing phones through participating carriers.

Intellectual property

The company's satellite architecture and related patents may provide some protection, although technology leadership must continue to be demonstrated.

First-mover advantage

Successfully deploying a working commercial network before competitors could create meaningful advantages in customer relationships and operational experience.

## What I'm paying for growth
This investment is based almost entirely on future expectations rather than current financial performance.

I am comfortable considering ASTS only if:

technology milestones continue to be achieved
commercial deployments expand
financing remains available on reasonable terms
management consistently delivers on stated timelines
evidence of recurring commercial demand increases

I become cautious if:

repeated execution delays occur
financing becomes highly dilutive
customer adoption disappoints
competitors establish a stronger commercial position

## Kill criteria (what would make me sell)
Do not initiate or continue the investment if:

Core technology consistently fails to perform as demonstrated.
Commercial rollout is repeatedly delayed without credible explanations.
The company requires excessive shareholder dilution to survive.
Key strategic partners reduce their commitment.
A competitor establishes a clearly superior commercial solution.
Management repeatedly misses major milestones.
Regulatory barriers materially impair the business model.

## Known risks
Significant execution risk.
Need for ongoing capital raises.
Technology performance risk.
Launch failures or satellite malfunctions.
Regulatory approvals.
Competition from established satellite and telecommunications companies.
Long timeline to meaningful profitability.
Customer adoption risk.
