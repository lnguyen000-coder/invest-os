# International Business Machines Corporation (IBM) — Investment Thesis



## Thesis in one paragraph
IBM is transforming from a legacy hardware company into an enterprise software, hybrid cloud, AI, and consulting platform focused on mission-critical business operations. I believe the market still associates IBM with its slower-growth past and may underestimate the durability of its software portfolio, the strategic value of Red Hat, and its ability to monetize enterprise AI through trusted customer relationships. While IBM is unlikely to grow as quickly as leading technology companies, it has the potential to generate steady free cash flow, expand software margins, and compound shareholder value through disciplined execution and capital allocation.  

## Moat
Enterprise switching costs

IBM software often runs systems that organizations cannot easily replace because of operational risk, integration complexity, and regulatory requirements.

Red Hat ecosystem

Red Hat strengthens IBM's position in hybrid cloud by allowing customers to deploy applications across multiple cloud providers and on-premises infrastructure without being locked into a single platform.

Trusted enterprise relationships

IBM has decades-long relationships with many of the world's largest enterprises and governments, giving it privileged access to strategic technology decisions.

Consulting + Software combination

IBM can help customers design, implement, and operate technology solutions, creating opportunities to cross-sell software and AI offerings.

Mission-critical infrastructure

Many IBM products support workloads where reliability, security, and uptime matter more than using the newest technology.

## What I'm paying for growth
IBM is not a hyper-growth investment.

I would own IBM expecting:

modest revenue growth
improving margins
growing free cash flow
disciplined capital allocation
increasing software mix
expanding AI revenues

I become cautious if:

software growth slows
Red Hat loses momentum
AI initiatives fail to generate meaningful enterprise demand
consulting margins deteriorate

## Kill criteria (what would make me sell)
Do not own IBM if:

Red Hat stops driving software growth.
Software becomes a smaller portion of revenue.
Free cash flow deteriorates for multiple years.
Management makes value-destructive acquisitions.
Enterprise customers begin replacing IBM platforms at increasing rates.
AI investments fail to improve long-term competitiveness.
Return on invested capital declines materially.

## Known risks
